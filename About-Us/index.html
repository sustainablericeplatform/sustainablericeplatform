OVERVIEW

The Sustainable Rice Platform (SRP) promotes resource-use efficiency and sustainability in the global rice sector through an alliance that links research, production, policy making, trade and consumption. It bridges institutional members across regions and sectors to find local solutions for rice smallholders that will help improve yield, reduce costs and protect the environment.

The SRP aims to encourage one million farmers to adopt climate-smart sustainable best practices in five years (2016-2020), as an active response to the global call to end poverty, protect the planet, and ensure prosperity for all, as set out in the UN Sustainable Development Goals.

MISSION

To promote resource efficiency and sustainability in the global rice sector through an alliance that links research, production, policy making, trade and consumption

VISION

“1 million rice smallholders adopt climate-smart best practice, boosting farm incomes and protecting our environment through an alliance that drives innovation and creates shared value”

GOVERNANCE

The SRP is co-convened by the UN Environment and the International Rice Research Institution. Each year, the SRP holds an annual plenary meeting and general assembly to review its activities and plan forward. Its overall operations is mainly supported by the following groups:

The Advisory Committee, provides an overall oversight of the Working Groups and the Secretariat. The AC is responsible for guiding work plans and ensuring that the SRP achieves its strategic objectives.

The Working Groups focus on implementation. Members institutions may contribute to more than 1 WG. The groups deliver on the mandate as deliver defined by the plenary and the Advisory Committee.

The Secretariat supports the Advisory Committee and the Working Groups. It is responsible for the administration and coordination of SRP’s activities.
